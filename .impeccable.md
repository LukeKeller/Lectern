# Impeccable Design Context — Lectern

## Design Context

### Users

Self-hosters and committed readers who run their own software. They already keep RSS feeds and a read-later pile, are frustrated by the split between feed readers and save-for-later tools, and run Lectern (YunoHost PWA) to own their reading stack. Contexts: fast triage of an overflowing inbox on a phone; focused long-form reading on tablet/desktop in the evening; quick saves from a browser share sheet. Job to be done: turn a scattered stream into one calm, trustworthy queue they can triage, read, and revisit — offline, on any device, without an algorithm.

### Brand Personality

Calm, literary, trustworthy. Emotional goal: the relief of a tidy reading queue plus the pleasure of a beautifully set column of text. Tone: unhurried, confident, never noisy or attention-seeking.

### Aesthetic Direction

Warm paper aesthetic: tinted neutrals (never pure black/white), one restrained ink-blue accent (≤10% visual weight), hairline borders, paper grain texture, serif-led reading typography (Iowan Old Style/Charter stack + bundled Literata, Atkinson Hyperlegible, Lexend, OpenDyslexic). Six themes (Paper, Sepia, Newsprint, Dark, Black, Contrast) + auto. Three reading surfaces: Full Article Reader (/read/[id]), Magazine (long scroll issue), Newspaper (two-column flip edition).

Anti-references:

- NOT a busy social feed (no infinite scroll, engagement bait, badges)
- NOT sterile corporate SaaS (no gray-card dashboards, cold default blue, Inter-everything)
- NOT flashy AI aesthetic (no dark-mode neon, glassmorphism, purple gradients, glow)
- NOT a cluttered power-user IDE (power exists but progressively disclosed)

### Design Principles

1. The text leads, the app recedes — every surface earns its ink by serving reading or triage; chrome stays hairline-quiet.
2. Reader sovereignty — the reader controls face, size, measure, leading, theme; defaults are excellent so controls are rarely needed.
3. Print lineage, screen craft — drop caps, folios, ornaments, and column rules quote print tradition but must serve on-screen legibility first.
4. Calm by default — motion is gentle (≤180ms, ease-out), accents are rare, nothing pulses or begs.
5. Accessibility is first-class — WCAG AAA where feasible, keyboard-operable everything, reduced-motion respected, legibility fonts bundled.
